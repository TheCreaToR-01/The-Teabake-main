Baking was always her quiet ritual. It became her life's work.

The Beginning

Rashmi's love for baking began long before her career did. As a child, she was fascinated by home-baked cakes. She would often request her aunts to bake one for her because nothing tasted like a cake made at home — warmer, richer, and more soulful than anything she ate outside. That warmth stayed with her.

There were times they wouldn’t bake it. And that's when something shifted — she decided she would learn herself. From that point, baking became her quiet ritual.

In 2020, in the quiet of her home kitchen, The Tea Bake was born. Her journey then took her to ENSP in France and ALMA in Italy for (classical remove) french and Italian pastry training, before returning to contribute to innovative eggless and vegan offerings at The Oberoi, New Delhi.

"Simplicity, she learned, requires mastery."
The Philosophy

At The Tea Bake, the approach is clear: no complicated flavours, no unnecessary drama, no chasing viral trends.

Just clean ingredients. Honest creations. Balanced, refined bakes that speak for themselves. Food that comforts before it impresses — flavours familiar yet elevated. Trust earned through consistency, not noise.

Focused on thoughtful tea cakes, artisanal breads, viennoiserie, and signature creations — mostly vegan and eggless — crafted with love and care.

The Vision

As The Tea Bake transitions from home kitchen to commercial space, the vision is not loud growth — but quiet excellence. To build something enduring. To create food that feels personal.

Because for Rashmi, baking was never about following trends. It was about recreating that feeling of home — and sharing it with others.

A Note from the Founder

I grew up believing that the best cakes came from home kitchens — made by hands that cared, with warmth that no patisserie could replicate. That belief shaped everything I've built here.

My years in aviation and hospitality taught me excellence lives in precision and quiet dedication. My training in France and Italy confirmed that simplicity, done right, is the highest form of craft.

At The Tea Bake, I don't bake to impress. I bake to comfort. Every loaf, every tart, every cake is made as I would make it for someone I love — with the finest ingredients, honest technique, and a whole lot of heart.

— With love, Rashmi
